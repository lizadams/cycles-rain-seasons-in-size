CYCLES & RAIN / SEASONS IN SIZE
By Andrew McNutt

This graphic tells a small story about bicycle traffic on the Fremont bridge in Seattle Washington through the course of 2018. It does so using a traditional graphic (a line chart), a fashionable but unusual chart (a sunburst), and novel chart type (the table cartograms nested into a treemap). The table cartogram (a visualization arising from, but considered only in passing by, the academic set) encodes tabular data through the areas of a grid while maintaining the adjacency of that grid, all while keeping everything bound within a rectangle. We make a table cartogram of each of the months in the year and them in a proportionately-sized slice-dice treemap. From this combo we are able to see a number of dimensions and granularities simultaneously: the relative popularity of travel during the weekend to the workweek, that no one rides during thanksgiving, and XYZ. The table cartogram is a close relative of the geographic cartogram (although cast into an abstract light), the popularity of summer compared to winter, and a host of small nuances.

This work makes use of some software I wrote for my Masters thesis in Computer Science at the University of Chicago. A further write up of that library, the types of graphics that it produces, and the nature of table cartogram usage is still forthcoming. For now: this graphic was produced by combining data published by the city of Seattle and historical weather data from Darksky. It makes use of a lot of custom javascript code (which you can view here) to calculate layout and positioning, react-vis for rendering, and sketch for visual tuning.
